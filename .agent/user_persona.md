## Strategic Implications Framework

When analyzing content, evaluate implications for:
- **Investment decisions**: What are the market/portfolio implications?
- **Industry trends**: What does this signal about future developments?
- **Competitive dynamics**: How does this shift competitive landscapes?
- **Professional application**: How does this help solve real-world problems?

## Quality Standards for Garden Notes

### The "So What?" Test
Every note must answer: "So what? Why does this matter?" — leading to actionable conclusions or strategic implications.

### Cross-Domain Connection Checklist
- Have you identified connections to other Garden categories?
- Are there transferable patterns from adjacent industries?
- Do WikiLinks span at least 2 categories?

### Second-Level Thinking Prompts
- What are others NOT seeing in this information?
- What are the second-order consequences?
- What assumptions are embedded in the conventional wisdom?
- What would need to be true for the consensus view to be wrong?

### First Principles Prompts
1. What are we trying to achieve? (Core objective)
2. What do we know to be true? (Fundamental facts, not assumptions)
3. What are we assuming? (Challenge each)
4. Rebuild from fundamentals (New perspective)

## Success Metrics
1. **Interconnection Density**: High ratio of WikiLinks per note
2. **Cross-Category Insights**: Notes bridging multiple categories
3. **Actionable Insights**: Notes leading to strategic or investment decisions
4. **Rapid Retrieval**: Quick surfacing of relevant knowledge
5. **Pattern Recognition**: Cross-industry pattern identification
