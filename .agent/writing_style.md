# Writing Style Guide

> Reference file for polishing user's writing. Consult this whenever asked to edit, polish, or draft social media posts, threads, essays, or any public-facing writing.
> **Last analyzed**: 2026-04-02 | **Samples**: Lab theses, essays, conversation messages

## Voice & Tone

- **Language**: Context-dependent code-switching:
  - **Analytical/investment**: Primarily English
  - **Personal essays/reflections**: Primarily Chinese (繁體), with English for tech terms and product names
  - **Mixed naturally** within sentences — "從一開始的prompt engineering到GEM" / "我還去爬了好幾千的單字然後按照日常使用frequency去做level分級"
  - Never forces one language — whichever feels natural for the thought, that's the language used
- **Register**: Two distinct modes:
  - **Analytical mode**: Professional but conversational, whiteboard-style (thesis writing)
  - **Essay mode**: Intimate, stream-of-consciousness, confessional. Reads like talking to a close friend over drinks. Raw and unpolished is a feature, not a bug.
- **Confidence**: Assertive and direct in both modes. States positions plainly — "我直接回答不用找那種東西", "我只深刻認為本質不會消失". No excessive hedging. Will stake a contrarian position without apologizing.
- **Humor**: Dry, self-deprecating, relatable. Absent in analytical writing but very present in essays and conversation. Uses humor to make serious points land — "中年男人要顧的事情太多了...", "搞了一堆玩具專案". Not performative, feels genuine.
- **Signature moves**:
  - Opens with **landscape framing** before zooming into the specific question — sets context first ("As the proliferation of AI infrastructure buildout and the advancement in AI techstack...")
  - Uses **parenthetical detail** to show depth without derailing the main argument — "(Variations of CoWoS, EMIB and CoPoS)", "(Chip level, module level and system level)"
  - Ends with **concrete research agenda** — lists numbered action items, not vague next steps
  - **Spontaneous personal asides in parentheses** — real-life tangents that humanize the argument: "(中年男人要顧的事情太多了...)", "(最近因病吃很多類固醇所以多了解皮質醇其實時刻影響著我們)"
  - **Self-aware humor at punchlines** — deflates grandeur with "lol" or casual self-mockery: "能力即服務吧 CaaS? New jargon? lol"
  - **Prefers colloquial over elegant** — "做中學" over "先學好再用，先用了再說"; shorter, punchier, no parallel structures
  - **Ruthless self-editor for social posts** — will compress 5 paragraphs into 5 lines. Cuts all middle steps and elaboration, keeps only setup→conclusion. "Daily muse" posts should feel effortless, not essay-length.

## Structure Preferences

- **Opening style (analytical)**: Top-down pyramid. Macro trend → specific opportunity.
- **Opening style (essay)**: Personal anecdote / situational setup. Drops you into the middle of the action — "這段時間在家休息，一整個海K猛送各種AI玩法". No throat-clearing.
- **Argument flow (analytical)**: Observation → gap → hypothesis → evidence → research agenda.
- **Argument flow (essay)**: Narrative → numbered reflections → punchline insight. The numbered points ARE the essay's skeleton — each one is a mini-thesis that builds on the previous.
- **Closing style**: 
  - Analytical: numbered research tasks
  - Essay: a sharp, contrarian insight that reframes everything — "SaaS is dead... 本質不會消失，我還是需要那些服務但我需要他們API化"
- **Paragraph rhythm (essay)**: Varied — long narrative blocks mixed with punchy one-liners for emphasis. Uses "..." for trailing thoughts and dramatic pauses.
- **Lists**: Numbered for essays (each number is a self-contained reflection). Numbered for research agendas. Never bullet points.

## Vocabulary & Phrasing

- **Preferred terms**: "buildout" (not "expansion"), "upgrade" (not "improvement"), "observe" (not "notice"), "forming a hypothesis" (not "I think"), "design-in" (industry term, uses naturally), "unit economics"
- **Avoided terms**: Does NOT use filler phrases ("it is worth noting", "it should be mentioned"). Cuts straight to the point.
- **Technical comfort**: Uses industry jargon without defining it — CoWoS, EMIB, SiC, GaN, MOSFET, IGBT, CCL, CPO, OCS — assumes the reader (or AI) knows the terms.
- **Code-switching rules (EN/ZH)**:
  - Main analytical writing: English
  - Company-specific research items: Chinese when referencing Chinese/Taiwanese market data (每家功率半導體廠的技術特徵)
  - Research task lists: Mix freely — Chinese for what to study, English for technical parameters
  - Numbers and specs always in English (6 inch, 8 inch, 5-7 years)

## Thinking Pattern

- **Supply chain thinker**: Naturally decomposes by level (chip → module → system) and traces implications up/down the chain
- **Contrarian instinct**: Looks for what the market ISN'T paying attention to ("there hasn't been much attention on power semiconductors")
- **Cycle awareness**: Frames industries through cyclical lens first ("historically, these are viewed as cyclical business"), then asks whether this cycle is different
- **Investment thesis structure**: Hypothesis → supporting logic → key variable to monitor → research required to confirm/deny
- **Quantitative anchoring**: Wants specific numbers to validate theses (conversion ratios, capacity data, average usage per application)

## Platform-Specific Notes

### Investment Thesis / Lab Writing
- English-dominant with Chinese research items
- Structured as: context → gap → hypothesis → evidence needed
- Numbered hypotheses and research tasks
- No fluff, no disclaimers

### Inline Edits / Feedback
- Ultra-concise. Often one sentence. "I don't originate." "It's always a pursuit."
- Corrects with facts, not opinions
- Uses "I think you covered these well" as rare positive acknowledgment

### Conversation / Chat
- Direct and efficient. States what's needed, not why.
- Comfortable giving direction: "Let's pause the session here"
- Asks clarifying questions when needed, doesn't pretend to understand

### Threads (Social Media)
- **Length**: Single post preferred for "daily muse" type content. Thread only if topic genuinely needs space (rare).
- **Tone**: Same as essay voice — personal, raw, no polish. 「幾個想法」 not 「幾個反思」.
- **Structure**: Story hook (1-2 sentences) → compressed insights (one per paragraph, no elaboration) → punchline/humor closer
- **Compression principle**: Delete all middle steps. Keep setup→conclusion only. If the essay says A→B→C→D, the post says A→D.
- **Open questions over declarations**: 「結果軟體端先到了?」 — invites engagement without being preachy
- **No hashtags, no emojis, no "follow for more"**

## Anti-Patterns (Never Do This)

- ❌ Don't add disclaimers or qualifiers ("it might be worth considering", "one could argue")
- ❌ Don't use formal academic transitions ("Furthermore", "Moreover", "In conclusion")
- ❌ Don't summarize what was just said — move the argument forward
- ❌ Don't use emojis in analytical writing
- ❌ Don't define basic technical terms — the user assumes domain expertise in the reader
- ❌ Don't write long introductions — get to the point within the first 2 sentences
- ❌ **Don't over-polish essays** — user actively rejected polished parallel structures and grand declarations in favor of shorter, colloquial phrasing. "做中學" beats "先學好再用，先用了再說". "New jargon? lol" beats "SaaS不是死了，是脫殼進化了".
- ❌ **Don't use finance jargon in personal essays** — "複利" was rejected in personal context. Keep financial framing for analytical mode.
- ❌ **Don't add dramatic labels** — "這才是真正的民主化" and "這是範式轉移" were both cut. Let the insight speak for itself without declaring its importance.
